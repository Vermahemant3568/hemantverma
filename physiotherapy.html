Physioterapy Website Project Introduction

About the Project

This is a physiotherapy website built to help users book appointments, read about services, and learn exercises.

  • Client: Physiotherapy Clinic
  • Role: UX/UI Designer & Developer
  • Tools: Figma, Adobe XD
  • Technology: HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, Laravel
  • Duration: 1 month

Challenges

Understanding user behavior and ensuring accessibility for all age groups was one of the biggest challenges. Designing a clean yet informative UI for diverse user groups was key.

Research

We conducted surveys and interviews with patients and physiotherapists to understand common pain points in current systems. Key findings showed users prefer simple navigation and faster booking processes.

Insights were synthesized into features like appointment reminders, video exercise tutorials, and profile customization for better user engagement and satisfaction.

User Personas

Persona 1: "The Proactive Pain Reliever" (Sarah, 38, Office Worker)

Goal: Find a reputable physiotherapist to address chronic back pain.
Needs:Clear information on specific conditions treated, practitioner qualifications, online booking availability, location details, and testimonials.
Pain Points:Difficulty finding clear pricing, unclear appointment process, websites that are hard to navigate on mobile.
Behavior: Uses search engines to find local clinics, compares services and reviews, prefers online booking.

Persona 2: "The Post-Injury Rehaholic" (e.g., Mark, 55, Retired Athlete)

Goal: Seek rehabilitation after a sports injury or surgery.

Needs: Detailed information about rehabilitation programs, specialized treatments, success stories, and therapist expertise in sports injuries.

Pain Points: Lack of information on specific equipment or techniques used, generic service descriptions, feeling rushed during initial inquiries.

Behavior: Seeks in-depth content, might call for initial consultation, values detailed information.
